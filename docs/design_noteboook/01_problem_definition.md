Crude Oil Preheat Train Digital Twin System

01_problem_definition.md — Problem Definition

---

1. Context

In crude oil refining processes, heat exchanger networks (commonly referred to as preheat trains) are used to recover thermal energy from process streams before the crude enters the furnace.

These systems play a critical role in reducing external fuel consumption and improving overall plant energy efficiency.

---

2. Engineering Problem

Over time, heat exchanger networks experience performance degradation due to operational and physical factors such as fouling, flow variation, and thermal inefficiencies.

This leads to:

- reduced heat transfer efficiency
- increased furnace fuel consumption
- loss of recoverable thermal energy
- uneven performance across heat exchanger networks

In many industrial settings, this degradation is not detected early due to limited system-level visibility.

---

3. Core Problem Statement

There is a lack of a unified system that can:

- model heat exchanger networks as an integrated system
- evaluate performance degradation over time
- quantify energy losses at system level
- provide interpretable engineering insights into inefficiencies
- simulate realistic operational conditions in crude oil preheat systems

---

4. Engineering Gap

Current industrial tools typically focus on either:

- individual heat exchanger design calculations
- or high-level plant simulation tools that are complex and not easily customizable

There is a gap between:

«component-level analysis and system-level operational insight»

This gap limits the ability of engineers to:

- quickly diagnose inefficiencies
- understand system-wide energy losses
- simulate degradation scenarios in a simplified environment

---

5. Motivation for the System

The motivation for this system is to develop a modular digital twin framework that:

- represents heat exchanger networks as interconnected systems
- simulates performance under varying operating conditions
- estimates energy recovery efficiency dynamically
- identifies degradation trends such as fouling effects
- supports engineering decision-making through structured insights

---

6. Target Users

The system is designed for:

- process engineers
- energy optimization engineers
- industrial operations analysts
- engineering students and researchers
- digital twin developers

---

7. System Goals (High-Level)

The system aims to:

1. Improve visibility of heat exchanger network performance
2. Enable system-level energy efficiency analysis
3. Simulate degradation behavior over time
4. Support engineering decision-making through structured outputs
5. Serve as a foundation for scalable industrial digital twin systems

---

8. System Boundaries (Problem Scope)

Included in scope:

- steady-state thermal analysis of heat exchanger networks
- simplified fouling behavior modeling
- system-level energy balance calculations
- performance degradation tracking

Excluded from scope:

- real-time industrial sensor integration
- CFD-based thermal-fluid simulations
- automated plant control systems
- full refinery process simulation

---

9. Key Engineering Insight

The fundamental challenge addressed by this system is not individual heat exchanger design, but:

«understanding and quantifying the performance of interconnected heat exchanger networks as a dynamic energy system»

---

10. Summary

This system addresses the need for a structured, modular, and interpretable digital twin framework for crude oil preheat train heat exchanger networks.

It focuses on bridging the gap between component-level engineering calculations and system-level operational insights.
